WAVE Partnership: Collective Action for Water & Biodiversity

Scaling collective action in global fresh produce supply chains – starting in Aconcagua, Chile.

Learn more Get involved

What is the WAVE Partnership?

WAVE – the Water, Agriculture, Values, and Ecosystems Partnership – is a global collective action initiative that restores ecological balance in critical catchments worldwide. It drives solutions at the farm level, within the enabling environment, and across the supply chain.

The WAVE Partnership offers a global yet locally grounded approach to tackling water and biodiversity risks. By moving from fragmented efforts to coordinated action, WAVE seeks to transform how we do business.

WAVE is founded by three organizations: The Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), Partners for Water, NewForesight.

What are the key barriers in scaling Collective Action?

The business case is unclear

  • ROI: Return on Investment for price, quality and product availability is not clear
  • Claims: Companies are hesitant to make sustainability claims due to fear of greenwashing accusations

…which limits effectiveness

  • Impact: Outcome-level impact of initiatives are piecemeal and not clear
  • Limited financial investments: Investments of max 5-10k/year solution for a multi-billion-euro problem

…and inhibits true scalability

  • High effort: High costs of coordination due to fragmented approach
  • Fragmented efforts: Approach to collective action is not widely agreed upon
  • Plan: No clear plan of action to scale other regions

Our Approach

Our Approach: A Catchment-Level Framework

The WAVE Partnership provides a scalable, catchment-level approach that drives solutions at three interconnected levels:

Farmers

Outcomes: Agricultural systems operate within ecological limits, enabled by water- resilient practices and supportive market conditions that contribute to rebalancing the water gap.

Supply Chain

Outcomes: Buyers strengthen sustainability risk management and due diligence while enabling market conditions that contribute to rebalancing the water gap.

Enabling Environments

Outcomes: Stakeholders collaborate through structured dialogue, with shared understanding, transparent data, and unified advocacy.

Our Process:
Four Phases of the WAVE Framework

We offer a four-phase, scalable framework adaptable to each catchment maturity level:

1. Scope

Define the situation, stakeholders, data, and risks to define the mission and align key actors on priorities.

2. Scope & Strategize

Develop solutions, define strategy, clarify services and value, and form a global-local partnership structure.

3. Organize and implement

Plan detailed actions, set governance, secure financing, and establish monitoring for implementation and learning.

4. Evaluate & Restrategize

Assess outcomes, gather insights, refine strategy, and prepare for the next cycle
